01 — Overview

What Digital Transformation
Means at Metamorphex

Most digital transformation programmes fail for the same reasons: they are led by technology vendors rather than business strategists, they underestimate the human and organisational change involved, and they try to do everything at once rather than delivering value in stages.

At Metamorphex, transformation is always strategy-first. We start by understanding what your organisation is trying to achieve commercially and operationally — and then design the technology architecture and change programme that gets you there, in the most practical and risk-managed way possible.

Our transformation practice covers the full stack: legacy system modernisation, workflow automation, cloud migration, API and data integration, ERP and enterprise application transformation, and the organisational change management that determines whether any of it sticks.

We are not a software reseller. We don't have a preferred platform we push you toward. We design the right solution for your context — whether that means a phased migration to the cloud, a targeted automation of specific high-friction workflows, or a ground-up reimagining of your core operating model.

02 — What We Cover

Six Pillars of Transformation

A comprehensive transformation programme draws on all six pillars — or we scope a focused engagement around the one or two that matter most right now.

01

Transformation Strategy & Roadmap

The architectural blueprint for your transformation — business-aligned, sequenced, and costed before a single vendor is engaged.

  • Current-state assessment and as-is architecture mapping
  • Business capability modelling and pain-point prioritisation
  • Target operating model design
  • Technology selection and vendor landscape analysis
  • Phased roadmap with business case for each initiative
02

Legacy System Modernisation

Replacing, re-platforming, or wrapping legacy systems without disrupting the operations that depend on them — the most technically complex part of most transformation programmes.

  • Legacy system audit and modernisation pathway assessment
  • Strangler fig and incremental migration patterns
  • API wrapper design for legacy system integration
  • Data migration strategy and cleansing
  • Parallel running and cutover planning
03

Process Automation & Workflow Redesign

Systematic identification and elimination of manual, repetitive, and error-prone work — using the right automation approach for each process.

  • End-to-end process mapping and automation opportunity scoring
  • RPA design and implementation (UiPath, Power Automate, Automation Anywhere)
  • Intelligent document processing and OCR automation
  • Low-code workflow automation (Power Platform, Appian, Mendix)
  • Process mining and continuous improvement instrumentation
04

Cloud Migration & Infrastructure Modernisation

Moving workloads to the cloud — or right-sizing an existing cloud estate — with the architecture, security, and cost management that makes it sustainable.

  • Cloud readiness assessment and migration strategy (6Rs framework)
  • Lift-and-shift, re-platform, and re-architect execution
  • Hybrid and multi-cloud architecture design
  • FinOps — cloud cost governance and optimisation
  • Cloud security and landing zone design
05

Integration & Data Architecture

Connecting the fragmented systems and data sources that currently prevent a unified view of the business — and building the integration backbone that future capabilities can build on.

  • Enterprise integration architecture design (ESB, EDA, iPaaS)
  • API strategy, governance, and developer portal design
  • Master data management and data quality framework
  • Real-time data streaming and event-driven architecture
  • Data lake and lakehouse design for unified analytics
06

Organisational Change & Capability Building

The human side of transformation — the part that determines whether a technically sound programme actually delivers. Change management, training, and culture shift that make new ways of working stick.

  • Stakeholder analysis and change impact assessment
  • Communications strategy and leadership alignment
  • Digital skills assessment and training programme design
  • New role and operating model design
  • Adoption measurement and sustained value realisation
03 — How We Think About Transformation

The Four Layers of a
Sustainable Transformation

Sustainable digital transformation operates across four interdependent layers. Technology alone is the least important — and the most over-emphasised. We design and execute across all four, simultaneously, so that capability gains compound rather than erode over time.

Most programmes that fail do so because they invest heavily in layers one and two while neglecting three and four. We weight our engagement design accordingly.

Layer 01

Strategy & Governance

Clear transformation mandate, executive sponsorship, governance structure, and a business case that connects technology choices to commercial outcomes.

Vision Roadmap Business Case
Layer 02

Data & Integration

A unified, trusted, and accessible data foundation — without which every downstream capability is built on sand.

Data Architecture APIs MDM
Layer 03

Process & Automation

Re-engineered, automated, and instrumented workflows that do more with less and generate the data that drives continuous improvement.

RPA BPM Low-Code
Layer 04

People & Culture

The workforce capability, mindset, and organisational design that determines whether transformation benefits are realised and sustained.

Change Mgmt Training Culture
04 — How We Work

Our Engagement Process

A six-phase model that keeps transformation programmes grounded in business value — and prevents the scope creep that kills most large-scale change initiatives.

01

Diagnose

Current-state assessment of systems, processes, data, and organisational capability. We produce a prioritised heat map of friction points and transformation opportunities.

02

Strategise

Target operating model design, technology selection, business case development, and phased roadmap. Governance structure and sponsorship model agreed before execution begins.

03

Architect

Solution architecture, integration design, data model, and infrastructure blueprint. Technical specifications reviewed with your engineering team before any build commences.

04

Build

Agile delivery in 2–4 week sprints, with working software shipped at each sprint end. Parallel change management and training running alongside technical delivery.

05

Deploy

Phased go-live with hypercare support. Cutover planning, parallel running, and rollback procedures designed before the first user touches the new system.

06

Sustain

Value realisation tracking, adoption measurement, continuous improvement governance, and knowledge transfer to ensure your team owns the programme after we leave.
